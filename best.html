Why should I use a genetic algorithm, and when is it best to do so?

Due to it’s use of crossovers and mutations the population of a genetic algorithm is more diverse than other algorithms allowing it avoiding stop at local optima.

What is a local optima? Imagine walking up a steep hill, as your walking up you can see the top but when you get to the top you realize there is another larger hilltop ahead. That spot you originally thought was top is a local optima, it's a local highpoint but not the actual highest point.

Therefor genetic algorithms are best picked in situations with multiple local optima.

Genetic algorithms are also the best choice to use when dealing with a large number of parameters.

Due to a large amount of leeway in how the programmer can decide how to the fitness score is decided, it can be used to take into account large amounts of parameters if needed. This makes them quite useful in all sorts of industries allowing them to take into account all sorts of variables that could affect the desired outcome.
